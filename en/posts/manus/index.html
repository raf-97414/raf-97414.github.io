🤖 Manus AI: Game-Changer or Just Overhyped? 🍗✈️

AI startup Manus launched with hype levels higher than a Taylor Swift concert. 🎤🔥
People are paying thousands for invite codes, calling it the next big AI revolution—but is it really?

🚀 The Big AI Promise

Manus claims to be an autonomous AI agent that:
✅ Buys real estate 🏡
✅ Writes research reports 📑
✅ Builds video games 🎮
✅ Orders food and books flights ✈️🍗

Sounds like the future, right? Well… not so fast. ⏳

🤔 What It Actually Does (or Doesn’t Do)

People rushed to test Manus, expecting AI magic. Instead, they got:

❌ Error messages & endless loops 🔄
❌ Missed factual questions 📉
❌ Broken booking links ✈️🤷‍♂️
❌ Food orders that never happened 🍔🚫

Even TechCrunch tried to get a fried chicken sandwich delivered.
10 minutes later? Manus crashed. 😭

🎭 Hype vs. Reality

So why did Manus go viral if it struggles with basic tasks?

📢 Scarcity & exclusivity – Limited invites made it feel special.
📰 Chinese media hype – Called it a “domestic AI breakthrough.”
💻 Fake demos – A viral video showed Manus using smartphone apps… but it wasn’t real. 😬

Even the company behind Manus admits it’s a work in progress.

🗣️ “We’re stress-testing the system and fixing issues as they come up.”

🏆 Final Verdict: Hype Over Substance?

Manus is still very early-stage—but right now, it seems more like:
🤖 A cool idea that doesn’t quite work yet.
🍗 An AI that can’t even order fried chicken.

Let’s see if they can fix the bugs before we start calling it the future. 🚀
